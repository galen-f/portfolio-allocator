Portfolio allocator

Allocate deposits (and optionally rebalance) across positions. Saved to this browser.

50%

0% = maintain current proportions exactly. 100% = allocate entirely by target weights. Blend in between.

60%

Shifts allocation away from positions at/above target price, toward those with more upside room.

50%

Blends two urgency signals per position: how far it is from its target weight, and how much upside/downside remains to its target price. Higher = price gap matters more relative to weight gap. Positions with no target price use weight gap only.

2×

1× = linear (all gaps treated equally). Higher values make the algorithm increasingly aggressive about the most off-target positions — large gaps get disproportionately more capital, small gaps get less.

0.6×

Scales the weight-gap signal for positions with no target price (typically broad ETFs/mutual funds). Below 1× = slightly deprioritised vs priced positions. Above 1× = treated as higher-conviction buys regardless of price signals.

25%

Base cap on how much of any single position can be sold in one go. Positions trading above their target price can be sold more aggressively — up to the full cap — proportional to how far above target they are.
